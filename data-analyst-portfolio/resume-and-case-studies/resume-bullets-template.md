# Resume Bullets: Templates & Examples

## Formula

`[Action verb] [what you built/analyzed] using [tools], [quantified result/insight],
[business impact or recommendation]`

Every bullet should have a number in it. If you can't find a number, you haven't
finished the analysis — go back and quantify the finding before writing the bullet.

Action verbs to rotate through (don't repeat the same one across all 7 bullets):
`Analyzed, Built, Designed, Automated, Identified, Segmented, Modeled, Forecasted,
Diagnosed, Quantified, Reduced, Improved`

## Fill-in-the-blank template

```
[Verb] a [artifact type] in [tool(s)] analyzing [data scope: N rows / N months /
N customers] to [business question answered]; identified [specific finding with a
number], informing a recommendation to [action], with estimated impact of [$ or %
if you can defensibly estimate one].
```

## Worked examples (one per project — copy, then edit with your real numbers)

**Project 1 — Retail Sales Dashboard**
> Built an interactive Excel dashboard (Power Query, PivotTables, Power Pivot)
> analyzing 3 years / ~9,800 orders of retail sales data; identified that the
> Furniture category carried a -X% profit margin in the West region driven by
> average discounts of Y%, recommending a discount cap that would recover an
> estimated $Z in annual profit.

**Project 2 — SQL E-Commerce Analysis**
> Wrote 15+ documented SQL queries (PostgreSQL) across a 9-table, 100K-order
> e-commerce schema to answer recurring leadership questions on revenue trend,
> seller performance, and repeat-purchase behavior; found a repeat purchase rate
> of only X%, flagging a retention gap the team had not previously quantified.

**Project 3 — Customer Segmentation (RFM)**
> Segmented X,000 customers into 5 RFM-based tiers using Python (pandas), finding
> that the "At Risk" segment represented only 12% of customers but Y% of historical
> revenue; recommended a targeted win-back campaign in place of a blanket discount
> email.

**Project 4 — Cohort & Retention Analysis**
> Built a cohort retention model (SQL/Python + Power BI heatmap) comparing user
> retention before/after a product change across X monthly cohorts; found Month-3
> retention improved from X% to Y% post-change, supporting a data-backed case for
> the redesign's ROI.

**Project 5 — A/B Test Analysis**
> Analyzed a checkout-page A/B test (~40K users, Python/scipy) including a sample
> ratio mismatch check and two-proportion z-test; found a statistically significant
> X percentage-point conversion lift (p < 0.05) and recommended shipping the
> treatment, with the readout memo adopted as the team's standard test-report format.

**Project 6 — HR People Analytics Dashboard**
> Designed a 2-page interactive Power BI/Tableau dashboard on 1,470 employee records
> tracking attrition by department, role, and overtime status; identified that
> employees working overtime had an attrition rate X points higher than baseline,
> surfacing a previously unflagged retention driver for HR leadership.

**Project 7 — Capstone: Supply Chain Analytics**
> Built an end-to-end supply chain analytics pipeline (SQL data model, Python
> demand/risk analysis, Power BI/Tableau dashboard) on 180K+ order records;
> quantified that X region/carrier combination accounted for Y% of late
> deliveries, recommending a carrier reallocation projected to reduce late-delivery
> rate by Z points.

## Rules of thumb

- 3–5 bullets total on your resume from this portfolio, not all 7 projects — lead
  with the capstone and 2–3 others that best match the job you're applying to.
- Never inflate a number you can't defend if asked "how did you calculate that" in
  an interview. If an impact estimate is hypothetical (you didn't actually change
  BrightMart's discount policy), say "estimated" or "projected," not "achieved."
- Match verbs/tools in your bullets to the job posting's language where it's honestly
  applicable — but don't claim a tool you didn't actually use.
