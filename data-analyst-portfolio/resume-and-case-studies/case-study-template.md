# Case Study Template

Use this for your GitHub project READMEs and any portfolio site. Aim for 500–900
words — long enough to show real thinking, short enough that a recruiter actually
reads it. This is also close to the structure of a good spoken "tell me about a
project" interview answer, so writing it well doubles as interview prep.

---

## [Project Title]

**One-sentence summary** — the whole project in a single sentence a non-technical
reader would understand. E.g., "Built a dashboard that showed a retail chain was
discounting itself into a loss on furniture sales in one region."

### The Business Problem

Who asked for this (a role, not a person — "the VP of Sales" not "my boss"), what
they actually needed to decide or do, and why the status quo (a static report, no
tool, a manual pull) wasn't good enough. 2–4 sentences.

### The Data

What dataset, its size/shape, and — importantly — its limitations. Real analysts
always know what their data *can't* tell them. One sentence on a limitation you
worked around or flagged.

### Approach

Not a tool list — a decision log. What did you try first, what didn't work, what did
you choose instead and why? E.g., "Initially scored RFM with equal-width bins, but
the Monetary distribution was heavily right-skewed, so I switched to quantile-based
bins." This is the section that differentiates you from someone who just followed a
tutorial.

### Key Findings

2–4 bullets, each with a number, each phrased as an insight, not a chart caption.

- Bad: "Chart shows sales by region."
- Good: "West region furniture sales carry a -8% profit margin, driven by average
  discounts of 22% vs. a company-wide average of 11%."

### Recommendation / Impact

What should the business do differently, and — if you can defensibly estimate it —
what's the impact of doing it? If this were a real engagement, what would you tell
the stakeholder to do on Monday morning?

### What I'd Do With More Time or More Data

One or two honest next steps. This shows you know the difference between "finished"
and "shipped a v1" — a distinction hiring managers care about.

### Tools & Skills

Short list — tools used, and the 2–3 specific skills this project demonstrates
(e.g., "quantile-based segmentation," "sample ratio mismatch detection," "DAX
measure design").

### Links

- [Dashboard link, if published]
- [GitHub repo/folder]
- [Notebook/SQL file]

---

## Tips

- Write the case study *after* you've done the analysis, not as you go — you'll know
  what actually mattered once you're done, and early drafts written mid-analysis tend
  to over-explain dead ends.
- Read it out loud once. If a sentence is hard to say naturally, it's hard to defend
  in an interview — rewrite it.
- Send me the draft before you publish it — I'll review it the same way I review the
  underlying analysis: is the "so what" actually there, or is it hiding in a chart.
